Fontana's Industrial HVAC Landscape

Fontana has evolved from its Kaiser Steel origins into a diversified industrial powerhouse. The city hosts one of the highest concentrations of heavy-use industrial facilities in San Bernardino County, with operations that run 24 hours a day under demanding thermal conditions. Understanding Fontana's industrial geography is essential to delivering effective HVAC service here.

Key Industrial Zones We Serve

  • Slover Ave Industrial District: The Slover Avenue corridor is Fontana's industrial core — a dense concentration of manufacturing, warehousing, and logistics operations spanning from the I-15 junction westward. We service multiple large-format facilities throughout this zone.
  • I-10 Freeway Corridor: Major warehouse and distribution center development lines the I-10 through Fontana. These Class A logistics buildings range from 250,000 to 1.5 million square feet and operate on demanding schedules with high cooling requirements during summer months.
  • Kaiser Commerce Center Area: The former Kaiser Steel site has been redeveloped into one of the largest industrial parks in the IE, hosting national logistics tenants and fulfillment operators requiring sophisticated HVAC management.
  • Citrus Ave / Cherry Ave Corridors: Mix of light manufacturing, food processing, and distribution operations that require specialized HVAC solutions including humidity control and production environment conditioning.

Major Employers & Facilities in Fontana

Fontana's largest industrial employers include Amazon (major fulfillment center), multiple national 3PL and logistics operators, food and beverage manufacturing plants, steel and metal fabrication facilities, and building materials distribution centers. Our technicians are familiar with the access requirements, dock configurations, and operational constraints of Fontana's major employers.

Industrial Environment Considerations

Fontana's industrial activity creates unique HVAC challenges. Particulate loading from manufacturing operations accelerates filter fouling and coil fouling. The city's inland location means extreme summer temperatures — frequently exceeding 110°F — that push RTU equipment to its limits. Our PM programs are specifically designed for Fontana's harsh operating environment.
