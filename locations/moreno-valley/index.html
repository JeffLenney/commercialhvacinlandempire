Moreno Valley's Industrial HVAC Landscape

Moreno Valley is experiencing an industrial development boom that will reshape the Inland Empire's logistics geography. The city's combination of available land, dual freeway access, and master-planned industrial development at scale has attracted the attention of every major logistics operator and industrial real estate developer in the nation.

Key Industrial Zones We Serve

  • World Logistics Center (WLC): The World Logistics Center is a 40.6 million square foot master-planned industrial development being built out in phases across approximately 2,600 acres of Moreno Valley. When fully built, it will be one of the five largest logistics parks in the United States. IE Commercial HVAC is positioned to support HVAC needs across WLC-area facilities with large-format installation capabilities and on-call emergency service.
  • MARVAC Industrial Corridor: The March Air Reserve Base-adjacent MARVAC (March Inland Port) area provides industrial development opportunities in a strategic location with access to SR-60 and I-215. Several significant logistics and industrial facilities operate from MARVAC, creating ongoing demand for commercial HVAC service and emergency response.
  • SR-60 / I-215 Interchange Zone: The confluence of State Route 60 and Interstate 215 creates Moreno Valley's primary logistics access point — connecting the city's industrial developments to both the broader IE market (via I-215) and the San Gabriel Valley / Los Angeles basin (via SR-60). Industrial development along these corridors represents a growing part of our Moreno Valley service area.
  • Amazon Fulfillment Campus: Amazon operates significant fulfillment and logistics infrastructure in Moreno Valley, including large-format fulfillment centers that operate 24/7 with strict environmental control requirements. Our team understands Amazon's operational demands and service expectations.

New Construction HVAC Opportunity

The scale of new industrial construction in Moreno Valley creates unique opportunities for getting HVAC right from day one. IE Commercial HVAC works with developers, general contractors, and tenants during the design and construction phase to specify appropriate RTU systems, plan service access, and establish PM programs that will protect equipment warranties and maximize system longevity from the first day of operation.

Cold Storage Growth

As the World Logistics Center develops, a significant portion of its capacity is expected to serve cold chain logistics — driven by e-commerce grocery, pharmaceutical distribution, and direct-to-consumer cold goods. Cold storage HVAC is substantially more complex than standard warehouse cooling, and IE Commercial HVAC brings the specialized expertise these facilities require.
