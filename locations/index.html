The IE Industrial Corridor

The Inland Empire's industrial network is defined by three major freeway arteries that connect our service cities into one of the most strategically important logistics markets in the United States.

🛣

I-10 Freeway

The primary east-west spine of the IE industrial corridor. Runs directly through Ontario, Fontana, Rialto, and Redlands — connecting the ports of LA/Long Beach to the entire Southwest distribution network.

  • Ontario — Fontana — Rialto — Redlands
  • Port of LA/Long Beach connection
  • Cross-country freight artery
🛣

I-15 Freeway

The primary north-south corridor connecting Ontario, Rancho Cucamonga, and reaching into Corona. The I-10/I-15 interchange in Ontario is considered the single most valuable industrial real estate node in the Western US.

  • Ontario — Rancho Cucamonga — Corona
  • Las Vegas / Phoenix distribution link
  • I-10 interchange mega-hub
🛣

SR-91 / I-215

SR-91 links Riverside and Corona to Orange County markets, while I-215 connects San Bernardino, Rialto, and Moreno Valley into the southern IE distribution network anchored by the World Logistics Center.

  • Riverside — Corona — Orange County
  • San Bernardino — Moreno Valley link
  • World Logistics Center access
