Why Standard Turf Fails in OC

Three Heat & UV Problems We Solve

🔥

Hot-Surface Risk

Crumb rubber and standard silica sand infills absorb and retain heat, creating surface temps 50–70°F above air temperature — dangerous for barefoot use and pet paws on any Anaheim Hills summer afternoon.

☀️

UV Fiber Degradation

Aromatic polyethylene — used in most budget turf — breaks down under UV within 3–5 years, causing color fade, fiber brittleness, and surface shedding. Coastal salt air and Newport Beach sun accelerates this significantly.

📐

Backing Failure

Latex-backed turf shrinks and cracks in OC’s thermal cycling (hot day, cool night), causing edge separation and surface buckling within 2–4 years — especially on south-facing Anaheim Hills hillside properties.

The Solution

Cooling Infill Technology

Our Cooling Infill uses hygroscopic mineral particles engineered to absorb ambient moisture from the air — including coastal humidity — and release it through evaporation when the surface heats up. This phase-change process actively cools the turf surface, maintaining temperatures within 10–20°F of ambient air.

Unlike water-activated systems that require a hose, the mineral infill recharges passively from morning dew and coastal air humidity — making it low-maintenance and always-on.

  • Reduces surface temp by up to 50°F vs standard crumb rubber
  • Recharges passively from ambient humidity — no irrigation required
  • Antimicrobial mineral compound inhibits bacterial growth
  • Compatible with all polyurethane-backed turf systems
  • Available as a standalone upgrade on existing installs
