Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know what RTU tonnage I need for my warehouse?

The general rule of thumb is 1 ton of cooling per 400–600 sq ft for warehouses, but the actual requirement depends on many factors: insulation quality, number and size of dock doors, internal heat load from equipment and lighting, occupancy levels, and climate exposure. We provide free facility assessments and detailed load calculations to determine the exact tonnage your facility requires before any equipment recommendation is made.

What is the 2026 EPA AIM Act R-410A phaseout?

The EPA AIM Act phases out R-410A refrigerant production starting January 1, 2025. New equipment using R-410A can no longer be manufactured after that date. Existing systems can still be serviced with existing refrigerant stock, but supply constraints will cause refrigerant costs to rise significantly over the coming years. We help facilities develop a strategic transition plan to R-454B or other low-GWP alternative refrigerants, including full documentation and reporting for compliance purposes.

Do you offer service contracts for multiple facilities?

Yes. We offer multi-site maintenance contracts with consolidated billing, a dedicated account manager, and priority dispatch. Many Inland Empire logistics operators manage 3–10 facilities under a single contract with us. Multi-site agreements also come with volume pricing and preferred scheduling for planned maintenance visits.

How fast can you respond to an HVAC emergency?

Our target is 2 hours from dispatch to on-site for all IE cities. We staff technicians across the I-10, I-15, and SR-91 corridors on Priority Commercial Response, and our service vehicles carry parts inventory for Daikin and Trane RTUs to maximize first-visit fix rates. Cold storage and food-grade facilities receive priority routing in our dispatch system.

Can you help us get utility rebates for HVAC upgrades?

Yes. We work directly with SCE and SoCalGas programs to identify rebate opportunities for high-efficiency RTU replacements, variable-frequency drives, and smart controls installations. Rebates can offset 15–30% of total project costs depending on equipment and program availability. We handle the rebate application process as part of our project management service.
