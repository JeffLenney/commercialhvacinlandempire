The IE Industrial Corridor

The Inland Empire is the logistics hub for all of Southern California — and increasingly, for the entire western United States.

The I-10/I-15/SR-91 triangle contains over 500 million square feet of industrial space, making it one of the densest industrial real estate markets in the country. This region serves as the primary distribution gateway from the Ports of Los Angeles and Long Beach, moving goods to the rest of the nation.

Major tenants operating in this corridor include Amazon, Costco, Walmart, Toyota Logistics, FedEx, UPS, and hundreds of mid-sized logistics operators and manufacturers. These facilities run 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, and operate under strict temperature, humidity, and air quality requirements.

HVAC failure in these facilities is not an inconvenience — it is a business crisis. Product spoilage, cold chain compliance violations, worker safety concerns, and regulatory penalties can result from even a brief loss of climate control. That is why the IE's largest facility operators choose a contractor who understands the stakes.

IE Commercial HVAC was founded in and for this market. Our service area is defined by the corridor. Our technicians live and work within it. Our response times reflect the geography. And our expertise reflects 15+ years of maintaining and repairing the HVAC systems that keep the IE's industrial corridor running.

Your 2026 R-410A Compliance Partner

The EPA AIM Act is the most significant regulatory change in commercial HVAC in a generation. Here is what it means for your facility.

The EPA AIM Act prohibits the manufacture of new equipment using R-410A refrigerant after January 1, 2025. Existing equipment can still be serviced using existing refrigerant stock, but with production curtailed, prices are rising fast and will continue to rise as supply tightens over the coming years.

Facilities with aging R-410A rooftop units now face a strategic decision: continue to repair and maintain existing equipment at increasing refrigerant cost, or plan a phased transition to new R-454B equipment that carries lower long-term operating costs and full regulatory compliance.

There is no single right answer — the optimal path depends on equipment age, condition, refrigerant consumption history, available capital, and available utility rebates. IE Commercial HVAC helps you build a data-driven transition plan.
